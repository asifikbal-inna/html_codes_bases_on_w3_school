Bangladesh (previously East Pakistan between 1947 and 1971 and East
            Bengal before 1947) is largely ethnically homogeneous, and its name
            derives from the Bengali ethno-linguistic group which comprises 98%
            of the population. The Chittagong Hill Tracts, Sylhet, Mymensingh,
            Barisal and North Bengal regions are home to diverse tribal peoples
            . There are many dialects of Bengali spoken throughout the region. 
            The dialect spoken by those in Chittagong and Sylhet are particularly
            distinctive. About (91.04%) of Bangladeshis are Muslims, followed by
            Hindus (largest-minority) at (7.95%), Buddhists (0.61%) and Christians
             (0.30%) and others (0.12%) as per 2022 census.
